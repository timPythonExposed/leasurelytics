Revenue Management Engine

From raw bookings and prices to practical commercial decisions.

This engine transforms fragmented operational data into forecast, pricing, and actionable guidance your teams can use day to day.

High-level pipeline

The structure follows the same logic as the implementation: clean, structure, unconstrain, forecast, disaggregate.

Scarcity and Hidden Demand Recovery

In business terms, sell-out means demand is still there but inventory is gone. We detect that moment, acknowledge hidden demand, and model unconstrained demand beyond that point.

Forecast with Confidence Bands

The x-axis is Week Before Arrival (WBA). Earlier forecasts typically carry wider uncertainty, while forecasts made closer to arrival are usually tighter.

Disaggregation to Action Level

We cluster many small groups into one larger learning layer for stable modeling, then decluster outputs back to smaller operational groups for commercial execution.

What happens under the hood

Based on the notebook workflow: from preparation and aggregation to unconstrained demand, forecasting, and disaggregated outputs.

Data foundation

We clean booking and product data, engineer features like booking pace and paid-price signals, then aggregate from market-group level to option level where needed.

Aggregation + Disaggregation

We aggregate options into clusters because this gives enough data density for stable learning. After forecasting, we disaggregate back to finer operational levels so teams can execute actions where they actually work.

Ideal Booking Curves

We build reference booking curves from well-performing historical patterns. These curves help recover hidden demand after sell-out and create more realistic forecast inputs.

Volume FC + Price FC

We forecast both future volume and future paid price in one coordinated flow, then disaggregate down to actionable levels so commercial and operations teams can act consistently.
